About Land Animals

Land animals, also called terrestrial animals, are creatures that live primarily on land rather than in water, using legs to walk, run, or hop, and inhabiting diverse habitats like forests, deserts, and grasslands, encompassing mammals (elephants, lions, dogs), reptiles, insects, and birds, categorized further as wild (tigers, bears) or domestic (horses, cows, pets). They possess adaptations like bones for structure (mammals, reptiles) or exoskeletons (insects) for life on land, requiring food, water, and shelter to survive.
