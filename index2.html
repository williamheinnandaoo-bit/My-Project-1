About Water Animals

Water animals are incredibly diverse creatures living in fresh or saltwater, adapted with features like streamlined bodies, gills (or lungs for mammals/reptiles), fins/flippers, and specialized senses for efficient movement, breathing, and survival in aquatic environments, ranging from tiny plankton to giant whales, playing vital roles in ecosystems like nutrient cycling. Key examples include fish (sharks, clownfish), mammals (whales, dolphins, seals), invertebrates (crabs, jellyfish, octopus, starfish), and reptiles (turtles).
