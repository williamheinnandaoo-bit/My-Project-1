About Animals

Animals are diverse, multicellular, eukaryotic organisms in Kingdom Animalia, characterized by their ability to move, consume organic material for energy (heterotrophy), breathe oxygen, and develop from a blastula. They range from tiny insects to giant whales, form complex ecosystems, and are broadly classified as vertebrates (with backbones) or invertebrates (without). The study of animals is called zoology, and they possess unique features like specialized systems for movement, sensing, digestion, and reproduction.
